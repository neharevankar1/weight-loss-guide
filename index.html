Foundation: How Weight Loss Actually Works

The Energy Balance Equation

At its core, weight loss is about energy balance:

  • Calories In < Calories Out = Weight Loss
  • Calories In = Calories Out = Maintenance
  • Calories In > Calories Out = Weight Gain

One kilogram of fat = approximately 7,700 calories

To lose 0.5 kg per week: Need ~550 calorie daily deficit

But it's not always this simple...

If you have metabolic challenges (insulin resistance, PCOS, thyroid issues) or chronic stress, your body doesn't follow this formula as predictably. That's what this guide addresses.

Understanding Your Metabolism

What is Metabolism?

Your metabolism is the sum of all chemical processes that convert food into energy. It has several components:

Component % of Total What It Means
BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) 60-70% Energy to keep you alive (breathing, heart beating, cells functioning)
Physical Activity 15-30% Exercise and daily movement
TEF (Thermic Effect of Food) 10% Energy to digest and process food
NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity) 5-15% Fidgeting, standing, daily activities

Why Some People Have "Slower" Metabolism

Your metabolism can be affected by:

  • Muscle mass: Muscle burns more calories at rest than fat
  • Age: Metabolism slows about 2% per decade after 30
  • Hormones: Thyroid, insulin, cortisol all affect metabolism
  • Genetics: Some variation is inherited
  • Dieting history: Repeated extreme dieting can slow metabolism

Good News

You can improve your metabolism through:

  • Building muscle (strength training)
  • Eating enough protein (highest thermic effect)
  • Consistent meal timing (body adapts, optimizes)
  • Quality sleep (repairs and resets)
  • Managing stress (lowers cortisol)
The Three Macronutrients

Protein

4 calories per gram

What it does:

  • Builds and repairs muscle
  • Makes hormones and enzymes
  • Keeps you full longer (highest satiety)
  • Stabilizes blood sugar
  • Burns calories during digestion (25-30% of protein calories used just to digest it!)

Why it's critical for weight loss: High protein preserves muscle while losing fat, keeps you satisfied, and actually burns more calories during digestion.

How much: For sustainable weight loss: 1.6-2.0g per kg body weight. For someone who is 84kg, that's 135-168g per day.

Carbohydrates

4 calories per gram

What they do:

  • Provide quick energy for brain and muscles
  • Fuel workouts
  • Replenish glycogen stores

The key difference: Simple carbs (white bread, sugar) vs Complex carbs (whole grains, vegetables)

  • Simple carbs: Digest quickly, spike blood sugar fast
  • Complex carbs: Digest slowly, stable energy

Why some bodies need less: If you have insulin resistance, your body doesn't handle carbs as well. Moderate intake (35-45% of calories) works better than high-carb diets.

Fats

9 calories per gram (more than double protein/carbs!)

What they do:

  • Make hormones (you NEED fat for this!)
  • Absorb vitamins A, D, E, K
  • Provide sustained energy
  • Keep you satisfied

Why you need enough: Too low fat (below 20% of calories) can disrupt hormone production and leave you constantly hungry.

Best sources: Olive oil, nuts, seeds, fatty fish, avocado, ghee (moderate amounts)

Why Your Past Diets Didn't Work

Common Diet Mistakes:

1. Too Extreme (Crash Dieting)

What happens: Eat 1000 calories or less per day

Result:

  • Rapid initial weight loss (mostly water + muscle)
  • Metabolism slows dramatically
  • Constant hunger, low energy
  • When you return to normal eating: rapid regain + more

2. Low Protein

What happens: Focus on reducing calories but protein intake drops too

Result:

  • Lose muscle along with fat
  • Metabolism slows (less muscle = fewer calories burned)
  • Always hungry (protein is most filling)
  • Harder to maintain weight loss

3. Inconsistent Timing

What happens: Eat at different times every day

Result:

  • Body doesn't know when food is coming
  • Hormones (insulin, cortisol, ghrelin) get confused
  • Especially problematic if you have metabolic challenges

4. Ignoring Sleep & Stress

What happens: Focus only on diet and exercise, ignore lifestyle

Result:

  • Poor sleep worsens insulin resistance
  • High stress raises cortisol (promotes belly fat storage)
  • Fighting an uphill battle

The Sustainable Approach

This guide teaches you:

  • Modest deficit (not extreme)
  • High protein (preserve muscle)
  • Consistent timing (optimize hormones)
  • Lifestyle factors (sleep, stress, exercise timing)
  • Flexibility for real life (social events, weekends)

Understanding Your Body's Challenges

Some factors make weight loss harder. Understanding them helps you work WITH your body, not against it.

Insulin Resistance

What is Insulin?

Insulin is a hormone your pancreas makes. Its job: move sugar from blood into cells for energy.

Normal Process:

Eat Carbs
↓
Blood Sugar Rises
↓
Insulin Released
↓
Cells Open, Sugar Enters
↓
Blood Sugar Returns to Normal

Timeline: 2-3 hours total

With Insulin Resistance:

Eat Carbs
↓
Blood Sugar Rises High
↓
Lots of Insulin Released
↓
Cells Resist! Don't Open Well
↓
Even MORE Insulin Needed
↓
High Insulin Stays Elevated

Timeline: 4-6 hours, insulin stays high

Why This Makes Weight Loss Hard:

  • High insulin = Fat storage mode: Your body CANNOT burn fat when insulin is elevated
  • Constant hunger: Blood sugar spikes then crashes
  • Low energy: Cells not getting sugar efficiently
  • More cravings: Body wants quick energy

How to Improve Insulin Sensitivity

  • Exercise (especially morning): Muscles absorb sugar without needing much insulin
  • Eat protein before carbs: Blunts blood sugar spike
  • Choose low glycemic foods: Slow, steady rise vs spike
  • Consistent meal timing: Body optimizes response
  • Lose weight: Creates positive feedback loop
  • Quality sleep: Poor sleep worsens resistance by 30%
  • Manage stress: High cortisol worsens resistance
Who has insulin resistance?

Very common! Risk factors: PCOS, family history of diabetes, excess belly fat, sedentary lifestyle, poor sleep, chronic stress, history of gestational diabetes.

Stress & Cortisol

What is Cortisol?

Cortisol is your "stress hormone" made by adrenal glands.

Normal Cortisol Pattern:

Cortisol SHOULD follow a daily rhythm:

  • Morning (6-9 AM): HIGH (wakes you up, gives energy)
  • Throughout day: Gradually drops
  • Evening: LOW (allows sleep)

This pattern is NORMAL and healthy!

Disrupted Cortisol (from chronic stress):

  • Stays elevated all day
  • Spikes at wrong times (night when should be low)
  • Never properly drops

Why High Cortisol Sabotages Weight Loss:

  • Worsens insulin resistance: Makes cells less responsive to insulin
  • Promotes belly fat storage: Specifically visceral fat (the dangerous kind)
  • Increases appetite: Especially for sugary, fatty foods
  • Breaks down muscle: Uses muscle for energy instead of fat
  • Disrupts sleep: Can't fall asleep if cortisol is high

What Raises Cortisol Inappropriately:

  • Chronic stress (work, relationships, financial)
  • Poor sleep (less than 7 hours, irregular schedule)
  • Intense exercise at night (should workout in morning)
  • Fasted intense workouts (body perceives as stress)
  • Extreme calorie restriction (body thinks it's starving)
  • Irregular meal timing (body uncertain about food availability)

How to Lower Cortisol

TIMING

  • Morning workouts: Work WITH natural cortisol peak
  • Consistent wake/sleep times: Regulates rhythm
  • Early dinner: Allows cortisol to drop before bed

STRESS MANAGEMENT

  • Breathing exercises: 5-10 min daily (activates relaxation response)
  • Boundaries: Learn to say no, protect personal time
  • Physical activity: Moderate exercise LOWERS cortisol (intense overtraining raises it)
  • Social connection: Talk to friends, don't isolate

LIFESTYLE

  • 7-8 hours sleep: Non-negotiable
  • Dim lights at night: Helps cortisol drop naturally
  • No work stress before bed: Wind down 1-2 hours before sleep
"You cannot out-diet chronic stress. Managing cortisol is as important as managing your diet for sustainable weight loss."
PCOS & Hormonal Imbalances

What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome - a hormonal disorder where insulin resistance triggers hormone imbalances.

The cascade:

Insulin Resistance
↓
High Insulin Levels
↓
Ovaries Produce Excess Testosterone
↓
Symptoms: Irregular periods, facial hair, acne, weight gain

Why PCOS Makes Weight Loss Harder:

  • Severe insulin resistance: Even worse than typical insulin resistance
  • Hormone imbalances: Affect metabolism and appetite
  • Inflammation: Creates vicious cycle
  • Irregular periods: Hormonal fluctuations affect energy and cravings

The Good News:

Weight loss with PCOS IS possible when you:

  • Address insulin resistance (primary focus)
  • Time carbs strategically
  • Prioritize protein
  • Exercise at optimal times
  • Manage stress and sleep

PCOS-Specific Strategies

  • Higher protein: 35% of calories (vs 25% for non-PCOS)
  • Lower carbs: 35-40% of calories (vs 50% typical diet)
  • Morning workouts: Capitalize on high insulin sensitivity
  • Consistent timing: PCOS bodies especially need routine
  • Specific supplements: Inositol, vitamin D, omega-3
  • Anti-inflammatory foods: Reduce the inflammatory cascade
Your Circadian Rhythm (Body Clock)

What is Circadian Rhythm?

Your body's internal 24-hour clock that controls:

  • Sleep-wake cycle
  • Hormone release (cortisol, melatonin, growth hormone)
  • Body temperature
  • Insulin sensitivity (KEY for weight loss!)
  • Metabolism speed
  • Digestion efficiency

Insulin Sensitivity Throughout the Day:

Time Insulin Sensitivity Best For
6-10 AM HIGHEST Post-workout breakfast with carbs
12-3 PM HIGH Main lunch with moderate carbs
6-8 PM MEDIUM Light dinner, fewer carbs
9 PM+ LOWEST Nothing (or very light if needed)
Important: The SAME meal eaten at 9 AM vs 9 PM produces a different insulin response. Morning = Better handled. Night = More likely stored as fat.

Why This Matters MORE with Metabolic Challenges:

If you have insulin resistance or PCOS:

  • Your baseline insulin sensitivity is already compromised
  • Eating at night = Double whammy (natural low sensitivity + your baseline resistance)
  • Timing becomes even MORE critical

Optimizing Your Circadian Rhythm

  • Consistent wake time: Same time daily (even weekends)
  • Morning light exposure: Within 30 min of waking (sets your clock)
  • Morning workouts: Align with natural cortisol peak
  • Consistent meal times: Body expects food at same times
  • Early dinner: By 7-7:30 PM latest
  • Dim lights after 8 PM: Allows melatonin production
  • Sleep by 10:30-11 PM: Optimal hormone reset

Meal Timing Strategy

Why Timing Matters:

Remember: Insulin sensitivity is HIGHEST in morning, LOWEST at night.

Strategy: Time your carbs when your body handles them best!

Ideal Daily Pattern:

Time Meal Macros Strategy
7:30 AM Wake up - Jeera/methi water, soaked almonds
8:15 AM Pre-workout Small carbs Banana + few nuts (quick energy)
9:00-10:30 AM WORKOUT - Exercise when insulin sensitivity HIGH
10:30 AM Breakfast 60p / 56c / 19f POST-WORKOUT! Carbs go to muscle, not fat. This is your LARGEST carb meal.
2:30 PM Lunch 60p / 85c / 20f Insulin sensitivity still good. Moderate-high carbs OK.
7:00 PM Dinner 35p / 40c / 10f LIGHT! Lower carbs (sensitivity drops). Focus on protein + vegetables.
10:30 PM Sleep - Body repairs, hormones reset

Key Principles:

  • Same times daily: Your body optimizes when it knows when food is coming
  • Largest carb meal post-workout: Breakfast after morning workout
  • Progressively lighter through day: Carbs highest at breakfast, medium at lunch, low at dinner
  • Early dinner: By 7-7:30 PM, gives 3-4 hour gap before sleep
  • Consistent! Even weekends (your body doesn't know it's Saturday)

Eating Order (Within Each Meal):

This simple trick can reduce blood sugar spike by 30-40%!

  1. Salad/Vegetables FIRST (fiber coats stomach, slows absorption)
  2. Protein SECOND (triggers satiety hormones, slows stomach emptying)
  3. Carbs LAST (by this point, absorption is already slowed)
Example at lunch:

Wrong order: Eat roti first, then chicken, then salad = Blood sugar spikes high

Right order: Salad → Chicken → Then roti = Blood sugar rise is moderate, controlled

SAME FOODS, different order, different insulin response!

Lifestyle: Exercise, Sleep & Stress

These factors matter as much as nutrition for sustainable weight loss.

Exercise Timing & Type

Why Morning Workouts Are Best:

  • Insulin sensitivity is HIGHEST in morning: Your body uses nutrients optimally
  • Works WITH cortisol rhythm: Cortisol naturally peaks 7-9 AM (good for energy)
  • Post-workout meal optimized: Breakfast becomes your prime carb-loading window
  • Won't disrupt sleep: Unlike evening workouts which keep you wired
  • Consistency easier: Get it done before day gets busy

Best Exercise Types for Weight Loss:

1. Strength Training (2-3x per week)

  • Builds muscle = Higher metabolism at rest
  • Improves insulin sensitivity more than cardio
  • Examples: Pilates, weights, resistance bands, bodyweight exercises

2. Moderate Cardio (2-3x per week)

  • Burns calories, improves heart health
  • Examples: Brisk walking, swimming, cycling, badminton
  • Duration: 30-60 minutes

3. Daily Movement (Every day)

  • Walking, stairs, general activity
  • Doesn't spike cortisol
  • Aids digestion, mood

Ideal Weekly Schedule:

Day Workout (9-10:30 AM) Evening
Monday Rest or light walk Light walk if desired
Tuesday Cardio (badminton/cycling) Gentle stretch
Wednesday Strength (Pilates/weights) Walk
Thursday Cardio Gentle stretch
Friday Strength Walk
Saturday Cardio or active rest Social time
Sunday Rest Gentle walk, cooking project
Avoid:
  • Intense workouts after 6 PM (raises cortisol when should be dropping)
  • Fasted intense workouts (cortisol spike, muscle breakdown)
  • Overtraining (more than 6 days intense exercise - raises cortisol chronically)
Sleep: The Missing Weight Loss Factor

Why Sleep is Non-Negotiable:

Even ONE night of poor sleep:

  • Insulin resistance worsens by 30%
  • Ghrelin (hunger hormone) increases 15%
  • Leptin (fullness hormone) decreases 15%
  • Cortisol stays elevated next day
  • Cravings for sugar/fat increase

Chronic poor sleep (less than 7 hours regularly):

  • Makes weight loss 55% harder (research-backed!)
  • Lose more muscle, less fat (bad composition)
  • Metabolism slows
  • Always hungry

Sleep Optimization Routine:

EVENING WIND-DOWN (8-10 PM):

  • 8 PM: Dim lights, stop intense activities
  • 8:30 PM: No more screens (or use blue light filter)
  • 9 PM: Relaxation activities (reading, stretching, family time)
  • 9:30 PM: Prepare for tomorrow (lay out clothes, prep bag)

PRE-SLEEP ROUTINE (10-10:30 PM):

  • Warm shower/bath
  • Skincare routine
  • Magnesium supplement (200-400mg, helps sleep)
  • Breathing exercise (4-7-8 method):
    • Inhale through nose: 4 counts
    • Hold: 7 counts
    • Exhale through mouth: 8 counts
    • Repeat 8 times
  • Read physical book (not phone!) for 15-20 min

SLEEP ENVIRONMENT:

  • Total darkness: Blackout curtains or eye mask
  • Cool temperature: 18-22°C (65-72°F)
  • Quiet: Earplugs or white noise if needed
  • Phone: In another room OR at minimum, face down, silent, far from bed

TARGET: 10:30-11 PM lights out, 7:30 AM wake = 8-8.5 hours

Sleep Non-Negotiables

  • Consistent schedule: Same bedtime/wake time (even weekends!)
  • 7-8 hours minimum: Not 6, not 6.5 - actual 7-8 hours
  • Dark & cool: Your body needs this to produce melatonin
  • No screens 1 hour before bed: Blue light blocks melatonin
Stress Management Strategies

Quick Stress Reduction Techniques:

1. Box Breathing (5 minutes, anytime)

  • Inhale: 4 counts
  • Hold: 4 counts
  • Exhale: 4 counts
  • Hold: 4 counts
  • Repeat 10 times

Use: Before stressful meeting, when feeling anxious, before bed

2. 5-Minute Morning Routine

  • Don't check phone immediately
  • Drink water
  • 3 deep breaths
  • Think of 3 things you're grateful for
  • Sets calm tone for entire day

3. Setting Boundaries

  • Work hours: Set clear end time (e.g., 6 PM)
  • No work emails after 7 PM: Phone on Do Not Disturb
  • Learn to say no: You can't do everything
  • Protect personal time: Schedule it like you would work

4. Physical Activity

  • Moderate exercise LOWERS cortisol
  • Walking, yoga, stretching especially good
  • Even 10-minute walk helps

5. Social Connection

  • Talk to friends/family
  • Don't isolate when stressed
  • Laughter genuinely lowers cortisol

When Stress Eating Happens:

The pattern: Stressed → Eat comfort food → Feel guilty → More stress

Breaking the cycle:

  • Pause before eating: Wait 10 minutes, drink water
  • Ask yourself: "Am I hungry or stressed?"
  • If stressed: Try breathing exercise first
  • If still want food: Choose protein-rich snack (keeps you full, won't spike blood sugar)
  • Examples: Boiled eggs, Greek yogurt, nuts, chaas

Stress Audit

If weight loss stalls despite good diet:

  • Are you sleeping 7-8 hours consistently?
  • Is work stress chronic (not just occasional)?
  • Do you have daily relaxation time?
  • Are you overtraining (exercising too hard/too often)?

Addressing stress might be THE factor that unlocks progress.

Supplements That Help

Core Supplements for Weight Loss with Metabolic Challenges:

1. Inositol (Myo-Inositol + D-Chiro-Inositol)

  • What it does: Improves insulin sensitivity, helps cells respond to insulin better
  • Dosage: 2000-4000mg myo-inositol + 50-100mg d-chiro daily (40:1 ratio)
  • When: Split dose - half morning, half evening
  • Who needs it: Anyone with insulin resistance, PCOS

2. Vitamin D3

  • What it does: Improves insulin sensitivity, boosts energy, reduces inflammation
  • Dosage: 2000-4000 IU daily (get blood test - many people need more)
  • When: Morning with breakfast (fat-soluble, needs fat to absorb)
  • Who needs it: Most people (80%+ have low D)

3. Omega-3 (Fish Oil or Algae Oil)

  • What it does: Reduces inflammation, improves insulin sensitivity
  • Dosage: 1000-2000mg EPA+DHA daily
  • When: With lunch or dinner (fat-soluble)
  • Who needs it: Anyone not eating fatty fish 2-3x/week

4. Magnesium

  • What it does: Improves insulin sensitivity, better sleep, reduces cravings
  • Dosage: 200-400mg daily
  • Best form: Magnesium glycinate (doesn't cause diarrhea)
  • When: 1 hour before bed (helps sleep!)

Supplement Schedule:

Time Supplements
Morning (with breakfast) Vitamin D, Inositol (half dose), B-Complex
Lunch/Dinner Omega-3
Evening (with dinner) Inositol (half dose)
Before bed (1 hour before) Magnesium
Important:
  • Start one supplement at a time (wait 1 week between additions)
  • Get blood tests for Vitamin D (know your baseline)
  • Quality matters - buy from reputable brands
  • Tell your doctor if you're on any medications

Real Life: Flexibility & Sustainability

How to maintain progress while living your actual life.

Weekend Flexibility Strategy

The Zig-Zag Calorie Approach:

Instead of same calories every day, vary by day of week while keeping weekly average on target.

Why This Works:

  • Prevents metabolic adaptation: Body doesn't "get used to" low calories
  • Allows social life: Can enjoy weekend meals without guilt
  • Psychologically sustainable: Something to look forward to
  • Still creates weekly deficit: Weight loss continues

The Math:

Standard approach: 1900 calories × 7 days = 13,300 calories/week

Zig-zag approach:

  • Monday-Thursday (4 days): 1750 calories/day = 7,000 calories
  • Friday-Sunday (3 days): 2200 calories/day = 6,600 calories
  • Weekly total: 13,600 calories (close to target!)

Practical Application:

Day Calories Strategy
Monday 1750 Slightly smaller portions at meals
Tuesday 1750 Same
Wednesday 1750 Same
Thursday 1750 Same
Friday 2200 Normal meals + social dinner (can have starter/dessert!)
Saturday 2200 Brunch with friends, normal day
Sunday 2200 Cooking project, relaxed day

Where the extra 450 calories go on weekends:

  • Restaurant starter (200 cal)
  • Small dessert (150 cal)
  • Glass of wine (100 cal)

Rules for Zig-Zag Success

  • Track weekly total: That's what matters, not daily
  • Don't go crazy on weekends: 2200 is still a limit, not license to binge
  • Monday-Thursday must be consistent: Can't make up for huge weekend overage
  • Protein stays high every day: Hit 166g even on high-calorie days
Eating Out & Social Events

Restaurant Strategies:

BEFORE YOU GO:

  • Don't arrive starving (eat small protein snack if needed)
  • Check menu online if possible
  • Decide in advance what type of meal (appetizer + main? Just main? Share dessert?)

ORDERING:

  • Start with protein: Grilled/tandoori chicken, fish, paneer tikka
  • Choose ONE carb: Naan OR rice, not both
  • Ask for modifications:
    • "Can the curry be made with less oil?"
    • "Can I get extra vegetables?"
    • "Sauce on the side, please"
  • Skip the bread basket: Save carbs for your actual meal

DURING MEAL:

  • Eat slowly, put fork down between bites
  • Stop when 80% full (not stuffed!)
  • It's okay to leave food on plate

Best Restaurant Choices by Cuisine:

Indian:

  • Good: Tandoori chicken, fish tikka, dal tadka, palak paneer (ask for less oil), grilled kebabs
  • Moderate: Chicken curry (tomato-based, not cream), biryani (small portion)
  • Avoid: Butter chicken, paneer butter masala, fried items, large naan

Asian:

  • Good: Grilled fish/chicken, stir-fried vegetables, tom yum soup, sashimi
  • Moderate: Thai curry (ask for light coconut milk), small portion rice/noodles
  • Avoid: Fried rice, pad thai, sweet sauces, fried appetizers

Italian:

  • Good: Grilled fish/chicken, salads, vegetable sides
  • Moderate: Pasta (small portion, protein on top, share with friend)
  • Avoid: Cream sauces, large pasta portions, garlic bread

Social Events (Parties, Gatherings):

  • Eat normally during day: Don't "save" calories (leads to overeating)
  • At event: Protein + veggies from buffet first, then treat foods
  • One plate rule: Load plate once, eat that, done
  • Hold a water glass: Keeps hands occupied
  • Focus on socializing: Not on food

PREPARATION:

DURING TRAVEL:

Breakfast:

Lunch/Dinner:

  • Order grilled protein + vegetables
  • Ask for olive oil on side (measure yourself)
  • Rice/roti: Small portion or skip

Snacks:

  • Greek yogurt (if available)

When Things Don't Go As Planned

Troubleshooting: Weight Loss Stalls

If weight hasn't changed in 2-3 weeks:

Check These First:

  • Are you hitting protein target? (166g daily)
  • Are you actually tracking portions? (Measuring vs estimating)
  • Sleep quality? (7-8 hours consistently?)
  • Stress levels? (High cortisol blocks progress)
  • Cycle timing? (Women: weight fluctuates with menstrual cycle)

Adjustments to Try:

  1. Reduce weekday calories to 1700 (keep weekends same)
  2. Add one more workout day
  3. Stricter restaurant choices (if eating out often)
  4. Check supplement compliance
  5. Evaluate stress management (often the culprit!)
Don't:
  • Drop calories drastically (below 1600 daily average)
  • Over-exercise (more than 6 days/week intense)
  • Give up (plateaus happen, they're temporary)
Troubleshooting: Always Hungry

If you're constantly hungry despite eating enough calories:

Check:

  • Protein at every meal? (Should be 40-60g per meal)
  • Eating high volume foods? (Lots of vegetables)
  • Enough fat? (Need 45-55g daily for satiety)
  • Drinking enough water? (Sometimes thirst feels like hunger)
  • Sleep quality? (Poor sleep increases hunger hormones)

Strategies:

  • Increase vegetables at each meal (adds volume, no calories)
  • Eat slower (takes 20 min for fullness signal)
  • Drink water before meals
  • Add fiber (isabgol before meals)
  • Check if you're actually eating enough (don't go below 1600 average)
Troubleshooting: Low Energy/Fatigue

If you're feeling tired despite following the plan:

Most Common Causes:

  1. Not enough carbs: You need 175-200g for energy. Don't go too low.
  2. Poor sleep: This is often THE factor. Prioritize 7-8 hours.
  3. Not eating enough total: Too aggressive deficit drains energy.
  4. Vitamin D deficiency: Get blood test, might need higher dose.
  5. Overtraining: Working out 7 days/week is too much. Need rest.

Solutions:

  • Add 25g carbs to breakfast (extra ragi chila)
  • Prioritize sleep above all else
  • Take 1-2 complete rest days per week
  • Get Vitamin D blood test
  • Consider eating at maintenance (2200 cal) for 1 week to reset
